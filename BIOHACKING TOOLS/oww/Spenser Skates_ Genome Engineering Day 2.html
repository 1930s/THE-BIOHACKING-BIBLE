Explore the legitimacy of the following statement: "synthetic biology is about engineering while genetic engineering is about biology." Synthetic biology and genetic engineering are different degrees of the same thing. Genetic engineering is the first step in taking biology from a descriptive science to an engineering discipline. What separates it from biology as a science is the concept of modification of a biological system to fulfill an artificial (human created) goal, as opposed to furthering the understanding of biological systems themselves. Synthetic biology takes this even further by attempting to create biological systems from base components instead of simple modification of existing ones. In this vein, synthetic biology is more about engineering than genetic engineering is, giving credence to the statement that "synthetic biology is about engineering while genetic engineering is about biology." I still disagree with this statement, however, because genetic engineering and synthetic biology are only different degrees of bringing biological science closer to engineering and fulfill that goal to different degrees.
