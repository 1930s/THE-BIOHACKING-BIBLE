A Wiki on Transcription Regulation is important. It's useful for your work: * When pubmed'ing: Unlike pubmed, our wiki will only treat work that concerns us. * When writing articles: You can quickly find and quote related work and can calm down reviewers. * When searching: Quickly find out if there is a website for your problem. * When evaluating: If several resources exists, comments indicate if they are good. * When being bored: You can discuss with other people that face the same problems as you. * When being frustrated by your supervisor: You can vent your anger by criticizing the work of strangers instead of your supervisor's one... :-)
