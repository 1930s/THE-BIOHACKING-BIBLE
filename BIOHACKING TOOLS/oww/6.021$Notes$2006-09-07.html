==Differential Equations== ''First order'': no terms of order higher than \frac{dy}{dt} (such as \frac{d^2y}{dt^2}) ''Linear'': no product of '''dependent''' variables such as y\cdot\frac{dy}{dt} ''Homogenous'': y=0 is a solution ===Solving first-order linear equations=== General form: \alpha\frac{dy}{dt}+\beta y = \gamma \rightarrow \tau\frac{dy}{dt}+y=y_\infty Solving: # Find homogenous solution first # Assume solution is y=y_{homo}+y_{non-homo} General solution: y(t)=(y_0-y_\infty)e^{-\frac{t}{\tau}}+y_\infty Only three things needed for all such 1st order linear equations: initial value y_0, final value y_\infty, time constant \tau. ===Example problems=== RC circuit, water tank, flux across cell
