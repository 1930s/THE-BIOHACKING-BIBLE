==='''3.1 Investigate optimum counting time for fluorodetector'''===
{| border="2" style="background-color:#ABCDEF;" align="right" | colspan="2" align="center" style="font-style:Bold; font-size:140%;" | Status |- | colspan="2" align="center" style="color:#9933FF ; font-style:Bold; font-size:120%;" | '''Results uploaded !''' |- | style="color:blue; font-style:outlined;" width="225" align="center" | [[IGEM:IMPERIAL/2007/Notebook/2007-8-21 | Planned for 21/08/2007 ]] | style="color:BLUE; font-style:Bold; font-size:110%;" | COMPLETED |}
'''Construct:''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_I13522 pTet-GFP]
A test to see what is the optimum counting time to have during our experiments. The counting time is the time the fluorometer detector stays on top of each well. A small window time will only account for very discrete levels of fluorescence. These might include sudden spikes of radiation since fluorescence is not a uniform process. Hence we will get variation between samples of equal expression rates. A larger counting time results in a larger window size and hence a more average reading is taken from each sample smoothing out the variation due to the randomness of fluorescence emition.
Care must be taken however because larger counting times will lead to faster fluorescence bleaching. A comprimise between the two must be found. {| border="0" align="right" style="font-style:Bold; font-size:140%; color:red;" | align="left" width="170px" | | align="right" style="font-style:Bold; font-size:110%;" | [[IGEM:IMPERIAL/2007/Experimental Design/Phase1/Protocol 3.1 |'''Protocol''']] |- | colspan=2 align="right" style="font-style:Bold; font-size:110%;" | [[IGEM:IMPERIAL/2007/Experimental Design/Phase1/Results 3.1 |'''Results''']] |}

'''Aims:'''
Determine the optimum counting time for the fluorometer. Avoid fluorescence bleaching.


'''[+] Constant Conditions'''
*Temperature: 25 oC (Room temperature) *200 Âµl aliquots from pTet culture in each well
'''[+] Variables'''
*Vary the counting time of the fluorometer from 0.15 sec to 1.05 sec. *The counting times tested will be : 0.15, 0.30, 0.45, 0.60, 0.75, 0.90, 1.05 sec
'''[+] Sampling'''
*Take readings every 5 minutes for 1 hour. Repetition: *4 repeats
'''[+] Controls'''
*Negative Control: E.Coli cultures without GFP expressing machinery *Positive Control: Diluted GFP in well
