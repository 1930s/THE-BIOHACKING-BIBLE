==Background subtraction== Subtract a media background, A_{media}, from the raw absorbance data, A_{raw}, and assume that the resulting data, A_{corrected}, is directly proportional to the number of cells in the well. {|width="720px" align="center" |width="100px"| |align="center"|\frac{}{}A_{corrected} = A_{raw}-A_{media} |align="right" width="100px"|...Equation 1 |} Subtract a fluorescent protein-free cell background, G_{cells}, from the the raw fluorescent data, G_{raw}, and assume that the resulting data G_{corrected} is proportional to the total number of GFP molecules in the well [''immature GFP?'']. {|width="720px" align="center" |width="100px"| |align="center"|\frac{}{}G_{corrected} = G_{raw}-G_{cells} |align="right" width="100px"|...Equation 2 |} ==Unit conversion== Use standard calibration curves (see here for absorbance and here for fluorescence) to convert the background-corrected data into absolute units (CFU/well and GFP molecules per well). The calibration equations used are shown in Equations 3 & 4. {|width="720px" align="center" |width="100px"| |align="center"|\frac{}{}CFU = 3.1e8 * A_{corrected} - 1.6e6 |align="right" width="100px"|...Equation 3 |- |width="100px"| |align="center"|\frac{}{}GFP = 7.0e8 * G_{corrected} + 6.0e11 |align="right" width="100px"|...Equation 4 |} ==GFP synthesis rate calculations== To calculate the mean synthesis rate of GFP per cell, S_{cell}, assume the total GFP synthesis rate is equal to the time differential of GFP. S_{cell} can be calculated as the total synthesis rate divided by CFU. {|width="720px" align="center" |width="100px"| |align="center"|\frac{}{}S_{total} = \frac{d[GFP]}{dt} |align="right" width="100px"|...Equation 5 |- |width="100px"| |align="center"|\frac{}{}S_{cell} = \frac{S_{total}}{CFU} |align="right" width="100px"|...Equation 6 |}
