'''yes''' #*Our project goal is to "knock out" the Sus1 gene of S. cerevisae's SAGA complex by "knocking in" a URA3 marker gene in its place. To do this, we have today designed forward and reverse PCR primers to bind to and amplify the URA3 gene. We have also given the primers 39 bp tails with sequence homology to the 39 bp flanking the Sus1 gene in S. cerevisiae, so that when transformed into S. cerevisiae, the PCR product can homologously recombine with the genome to insert URA3 and delete SUS1. #Day 2: Yeast transformation --in M&M? '''yes''' #*Today we transformed our Day 1 PCR product, conveniently, into a URA minus strain of yeast and plated the mixture onto SC-ura plates to prevent the growth of any colonies that did NOT homologously recombine with URA3.
