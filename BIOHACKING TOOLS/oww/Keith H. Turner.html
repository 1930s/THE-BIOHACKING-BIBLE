Apicella, M.D.]=== In the Apicella lab, I worked briefly on cloning [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisella_tularensis ''Francisella tularensis''] genes for cell division and LPS biogenesis, but the main bulk of my work there was on non-typeable [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haemophilus_influenzae ''Haemophilus influenzae'']. NTHi is an obligate colonizer of the human upper respiratory tract, and is carried by about 80% of the population asymptomatically. However, in patients with [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COPD chronic obstructive pulmonary disorder] or other immunocompromising conditions, it can cause acute episodes of bronchitis. The basis for its high carriage rates and persistence in infection is in its use of ''N''-acetylneuraminic acid (Neu5Ac), or [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sialic_acid sialic acid], for decorating its [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipopolysaccharides lipooligosaccharide] to evade the host [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Innate_immunity innate immune response]. As the bacterium lacks a Neu5Ac synthase gene, it depends on scavenging free Neu5Ac from the human host, a process which is mediated by the SiaP/SiaT Neu5Ac TRAP transporter Allen-IandI-2005. SiaP is a [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Periplasm periplasmic] protein that binds Neu5Ac and enables transport through the SiaT inner membrane transporter. My work centered around characterizing the SiaP-Neu5Ac interaction quantitatively. It was a great project that taught me a lot about microbiological and biochemical procedures, as well as more general things about performing and communicating research.
