Moreover, it involved both synthesis and assembly. #Codon Devices #Experience #*Codon Devices had the cheapest quote for this order with prices as advertised on their website ($0.79/bp; $500 surcharge for clones over 1.5kb; and a surcharge for a custom ''E. coli'' strain). GENEART was only slightly more expensive. DNA 2.0 was significantly more expensive. #*Delivery was estimated at 30-40 days. The 4 resistance markers took 6 weeks. The pSC101 replication origin took 8 weeks. The vector scaffold took 5 months. #*The resistance markers and pSC101 replication origin were synthesized as ordered. The vector scaffold had some mutations (some due to their issues during synthesis and some possibly due to my design flaws). Codon Devices opted to not pursue the project further at this point and did not deliver on the 3 vectors. [Additional note: It turns out that the vector scaffold they delivered was actually two vector copies fused together in such a way that when cut by any single enzyme, it looked the right size. Only running the uncut vector on the gel revealed the unusually large fragment size.] #Codon Devices was very helpful as I set up and submitted this order which was critical since it was a complicated project. Over the course of the order, the technical team was responsive to questions and suggestions. Codon Devices' decision not to finish the order was frustrating (though they didn't charge for that part of the order). They didn't respond to an emailed complaint on this issue.
