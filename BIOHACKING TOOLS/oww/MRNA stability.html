==What is the half-life of a typical mRNA in E. coli?== [[Jennifer Braff]] and [[Caitlin Conboy]] in the [[Endy|Endy Lab]] at [http://web.mit.edu MIT] measured the half-lives of some transcripts in [[E._coli_genotypes|E. coli MC4100]] in a [[Endy:STN_Chemostat_Protocol|chemostat]]. This work was part of the [[Cconboy: Soup to Nuts Project Description|Soup to nuts project]]. They measured mRNA half-lives for the following [http://parts.mit.edu BioBrick] parts - {|{{Table}} !Construct !mRNA Half-life (min) !mRNA degradation rate (s-1) |- |pSB4A3-I7100 |3.13±0.48 | |- |pSB3K3-I7100 |5.36±2.07 | |- |pSB4A3-I7101 |2.19±0.28 | |- |pSB3K3-I7101 |2.24±0.33 |5.2E-3 |- |pSB4A3-I7107 |0.98±0.04 |0.012±0.0005 |- |pSB3K3-I7107 |2.18±0.34 |5.3E-3±0.81E-3 |- |pSB4A3-I7109 |1.55±0.18 |0.0075±0.0009 |- |pSB3K3-I7109 |3.08±0.65 |3.8E-3±0.79E-3 |} From the above data, we can calculate a mean value of 4.8E-3 for the three devices using B0032 on pSB3k3 (I7101, I7107, and I7109). These data are consistent with measured half-lives for endogenous ''E. coli'' mRNA Bernstein. Bernstein ''et al.'' report that 80% of ''E. coli'' mRNA half-lives are between 3 and 8 min. Bernstein ''et al.'' measured a global average mRNA half-life for ''E. coli'' of 5.7 min. ==References== #Bernstein pmid=12119387
