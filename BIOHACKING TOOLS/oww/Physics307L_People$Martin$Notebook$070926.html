All we need to do is to connect our digital multimeter to the output plug ins on the back of the h/e apparatus. ---- ==Purpose== The purpose of our experiment is to determine the value of plank's constant (h). We will do this by examining the photoelectric effect and using the equation E=hf=KE+W where f is the frequency of the incident light, W is the work function (amount of energy required to eject an electron from the metal plate, h is plank's constant and KE is the max allowed kinetic energy for the ejected electrons. ---- ==Measurements== * note we are measuring the stopping potential for light of a given frequency. The stopping potential is the potential that is required to stop electrons from jumping and creating a current. Measuring this gives us a linear relationship between f and this stopping potential.
