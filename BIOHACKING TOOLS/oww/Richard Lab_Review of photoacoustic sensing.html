The wave length range is created by passing the IR light through a filter. The IR pulse causes the gases volume to change at every pulse, creating a pressure change pattern detected by a microphone placed in the sample chamber. The resulting sound wave varies with the concentration of the measured gas, which is interpolated through a calibration curve. In the Innova gas monitor (Innova, DK), the calibration curve is programmed into the sensor and the gas concentration is automatically output. || . |- | High sensitivity in gas analysis with photoacoustic detection.
