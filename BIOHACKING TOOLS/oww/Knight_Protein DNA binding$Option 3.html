MgCl2 is very hygroscopic. May want to order a fresh bottle since bottles should not be stored for a long time. ===4M potassium chloride=== To make 100mL *Dissolve 29.82 g solid KCl in 60 mL H2O. *Adjust volume to 100 mL with H2O. *Autoclave for 20 minutes on liquid cycle and store at room temperature. *Aliquot into 100 μL aliquots in sterile tubes and use one at a time. Note that this concentration of potassium chloride solution is near the solubility limits for potassium chloride in water. So you may need to heat slightly to help the salt go into solution. ===20mM zinc sulfate=== To make 100mL *Dissolve 0.575 g zinc sulphate heptahydrate in 60mL H2O. *Adjust volume to 100 ml with deionized water. *Filter sterilize?
