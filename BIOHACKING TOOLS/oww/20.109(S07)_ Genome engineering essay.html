To engineer novel biological systems, we need to change the genetic code of existing biological materials, not by making a few changes as current methods allow us to do but rather by making lots and lots of changes in a fast, cheap and reliable way. Just as “plug-ins” provide new or improved functions to existing computer programs, the current tools of molecular biology allow for piecemeal modification to genetic programs, adding functionality but often complexity and clumsiness as well. In this essay I will describe two approaches to biological programming, ad hoc adjustment and complete refactoring, as applied to the simple genome of the bacteriophage M13.
