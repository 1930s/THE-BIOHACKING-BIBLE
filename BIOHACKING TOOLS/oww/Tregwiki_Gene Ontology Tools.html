There are probably hundreds of GO tools. I don't know many. Have a look at [http://www.geneontology.org/ the Genontology Website for a better overview.] [http://crfb.univ-mrs.fr/GOToolBox/index.php gostattoolbox] most accessed article of genome biology, used by a [http://genomebiology.com.gate1.inist.fr/2006/7/6/319 recent paper] [ftp://ftp.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/DATA/ NCBI Entrez Genes GO data] in an easy tab-delimited format for simple loading into script-languages (mentioned by alexander morgan on the biopython list) there is a similar thing for plants somewhere on tigr.org. [http://wego.genomics.org.cn/cgi-bin/wego/index.pl Wego:] Upload your gene lists and see which category or sub-category is over-represented. The page has a good linklist for GO and graphics-tools. [http://gostat.wehi.edu.au/ Gostat:] Seems to be the same thing as Wego...? [http://david.niaid.nih.gov/david/ease.htm ease] Genebank to overrepresented category tool [[http://katahdin.cshl.org:9331/GO/GO.html GObar]] Sort Gene lists by Gene Ontology anntations [[http://www.charite.de/ch/medgen/ontologizer/ The Ontologizer] can apprently better cope with the complex parent-child distributions of GO [[http://func.eva.mpg.de/ FUNC]] claims to be better in its statistical approach than Gostat, is a lot more recent and, est of all, available as a standalone PERL-Program. In addition, it's author is very helpful.
