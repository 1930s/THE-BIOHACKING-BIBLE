This is the list of required parts that can be obtained from the registry and are needed for the project. ===Promoters=== List input promoters, find/measure PoPS in of the following promoters: *'''LuxR Promoter''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_F2620 BBa_F2620] Short description: A transcription factor [LuxR] (''[http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_C0062 C0062]'') that is active in the presence of cell-cell signaling molecule [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/3OC6HSL 3OC6HSL] is controlled by a tetR regulatable operator (''[http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_R0040 BBa_R0040]''). ''Device input'' is [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/3OC6HSL 3OC6HSL]. ''Device output'' is [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/PoPS PoPS] from a LuxR-regulated operator. If used in a cell containing TetR then a second input signal such as [[ATc|aTc]] can be used to produce a logical AND function. *'''Constitutive promoter family''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_J23119 BBa_J23119 ] Short desccription: Parts J23100 through J23119 are a family of constitutive promoter parts isolated from a small combinatorial library. J23119 is the "consensus" promoter sequence and the strongest member of the family. Part J23119 is present in pSB1A2. *'''Promoter P(Lac) IQ''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_I14032 BBa_I14032 ] Short description: Constitutive Promoter, High Transcription *'''Promoter (tetR, negative)''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_R0040 BBa_R0040 ] Short description: Medium strength promoter. Sequence for pTet inverting regulator driven by the TetR protein. Action is inhibited by the addition of tetracycline or its analog, aTc. *'''Promoter (lambda cI regulated)''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_R0051 BBa_R0051] Short description: The cI regulated promoter is based on the pR promoter from bacteriophage lambda. The promoter has two DNA binding sites for lambda cI repressor BBa_C0051. cI binding results in repression of transcription. The specific sequence used here is based on the cI repressible promoter used in the Elowitz repressilator (and references therein). *'''CMV promoter''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_J52034 BBa_J52034] Short description: CMV promoter with RBS region, used for testing of our reporter constructs. CMV promotor is a constitutive promotor. ===Ribosome Binding Sites=== *'''RBS (Elowitz)''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_B0034 BBa_B0034] Short description: This is the most efficient RBS in the registry, and sets the 1.0 efficiency standard. It is based on the Elowitz repressilator. ===Terminators=== *'''Double terminator (B0010-B0012)''' [http://parts.mit.edu/registry/index.php/Part:BBa_B0015 BBa_B0015] Short description: Double terminator consisting of BBa_B0010 and BBa_B0012. This is the most commonly used terminator. It seems to be reliable. forward_efficiency: 0.984 reverse_efficiency: 0.295 ===Parts Not in the Registry (To Characterize)=== *'''HrpR''' *'''HrpS''' *'''HrpV''' *'''''pHrpL''''' Further parts: *'''σL (HrpL)''' *'''Hrp Box'''
