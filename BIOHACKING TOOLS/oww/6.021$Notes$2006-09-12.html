==Review== * Fick's first law: \phi(x,t)=-D\frac{\partial c(x,t)}{\partial x} ** Flux is proportional to the concentration gradient * Continuity equation: -\frac{\partial\phi(x,t)}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial c(x,t)}{\partial t} ** A flux gradient leads to change in concentration over time * Diffusion equation: \frac{\partial c(x,t)}{\partial t} = D\frac{\partial^2 c(x,t)}{\partial x^2} * Steady state solution: time-invariant (\frac{\partial c}{\partial t}=0, \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}=0) ** \phi(x,t)=\phi_0 ** c(x,t) = -\frac{\phi_0}{D}X+\alpha * Equilibrium: Zero flux + time invariant **\phi(x,t)=0, C(x,t)=constant (uniform distribution) * Impulse response: Gaussian c(x,t)=\frac{n_0}{\sqrt{4\pi Dt}}e^{-x^2/(4Dt)} ** x^2 \approx Dt
