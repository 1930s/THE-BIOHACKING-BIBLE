{| | rowspan=2 | [[Image:Igem2006logo.gif|150px]] | '''iGEM''' is the international genetically engineered machines competition. The objective of the competition is to design and build an engineered biological system using DNA. Systems will be constructed from [http://parts.mit.edu standard biological parts]. Although iGEM originated at MIT, in 2006, there were around [http://parts2.mit.edu/wiki/index.php/Schools_Participating_in_iGEM_2006 40 schools] participating from around the world ([http://parts.mit.edu/wiki/index.php/World_map see map]). You can read more about the competition [http://icampus.mit.edu/projects/iGem.shtml here] or read a short description of what the [[/Blurb/|MIT team]] did. Our project Eau d'E coli won [http://www.igem2006.com/results.htm Best System] at the 2006 iGEM Jamboree! |- | [https://giving.mit.edu/givenow/AddGift.dyn?desig=2738692 https://giving.mit.edu/images/give_now.gif] ''' to the MIT 2007 iGEM team or alternatively,''' [[../Sponsorship |'''learn more''']]. |}
