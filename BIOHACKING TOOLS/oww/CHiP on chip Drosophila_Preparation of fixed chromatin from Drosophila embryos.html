(15−03−2006) ==Overview== The aim of the method is to isolate fixed chromatin from Drosophila embryos. Essentially, the chromatin is fixed, the nuclei are then purified and lysed before the chromatin is fragmented by sonication. Our embryo collections are typically performed over a 16 hour time span, 1−2 grams of embryos were collected for each chromatin prep. This protocol was developed by Rob White's laboratory. ==Equipment and reagents== * Sigma 4K10 bench top centrifuge * Standard microfuge tube bench top centrifuge * Formaldehyde (40% solution) 'Analar grade' (BDH; Cat. No. 101134A) * n−Heptane 'Analar grade' (BDH; Cat. No. 103636C) * 50 ml Falcon tubes * 13.5 ml screw−capped conical base tubes (Bibby Sterlin; Cat.
