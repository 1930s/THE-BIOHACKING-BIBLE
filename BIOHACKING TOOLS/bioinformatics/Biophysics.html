Molecular biology itself [http://nsm1.utdallas.edu/bioweb/History/History.htm grew out of biophysics]. The [http://www.cryst.bbk.ac.uk/BBS/ British Biophysical Society] defines biophysics as:
"an interdisciplinary field which applies techniques from the physical sciences to understanding biological structure and function"
[http://www.cryst.bbk.ac.uk/BBS/whatis/whatis.html More information about the various facets of the discipline] can be found at the [http://www.cryst.bbk.ac.uk/BBS/ society's site] hosted at [http://www.bbk.ac.uk/ Birkbeck College], London. Mike Goodrich wrote to ask what the status of biophysics was given the definition of computational biology submitted by Paul Schulte (below). A [http://www.the-scientist.com/yr2002/dec/prof3_021209.html recent article] in [http://www.the-scientist.com/ The Scientist] [free registration required] dealt with this question---thanks to Jo Wixon (Managing Editor of [http://www.wiley.co.uk/wileychi/genomics/cfg.html Comparative and Functional Genomics]) for the reference.
