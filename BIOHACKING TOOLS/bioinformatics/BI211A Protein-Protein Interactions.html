==Objective== With systems biology budding as a discipline, there is a colossal need for biologists to understand the interactions within biological networks. There has been much talk about systems biology and protein-protein interactions (PPI), and this course will dig into some of the fundamental issues concerning PPIs, their need and usage. The exercises and take-home messages will encourage students to give some thought to the topics covered and hopefully build some excitement about the field of systems biology. Nevertheless, this course shouldnâ€™t be considered too difficult for beginners. After completing the course, students should be able to understand and find answers to the following questions: * Whither systems biology? * Why PPIs? What else does systems biology involve? * What has made systems biology distinct from bioinformatics? * Would PPIs bring out a function for umpteen orphan genes? * What are the types of interaction data and their layouts? * What are the online tools for analyzing networks? * How are high-throughput methods used to measure interactions? * How are interactions validated? The student should have a degree in biology or computer science. However, this course covers diverse subjects, and so any graduate with adequate knowledge of biology and computer science may also wish to consider it. Please come prepared with the name of a gene that interests you, preferentially from the human genome. And also take a note of its transcripts and accession numbers. If you are unsure about which gene to choose, we will leave it as a pro tem for exercises during fourth day. We also suggest that students download Cytoscape from http://cytoscape.org/ with the MCODE plugin. The following websites will help the student become acclimated to the subject: * http://www.genecards.org/ * http://www.ihop-et.org/UniPub/iHOP/ * http://string.embl.de/ * http://mint.bio.uniroma2.it/mint/Welcome.do/ * http://www.ebi.ac.uk/intact/ * http://www.bind.ca/ * http://www.prebind.bind.ca/ * http://www.hprd.org/ * http://www.cytoscape.org/ This course is certified by the [[Bioinformatics Organization|Bioinformatics Organization, Inc.]], the largest international affiliation in the field, and it will count as '''5''' "Continuing Scientific Education" (CSE) credits (one credit per contact hour) within the Organization. Students completing the course will receive a certificate attesting to that. ==Course outline== * Day 1 ** Bioinformatics to systems biology: essential ascent ** The what and the hows of PPIs: a computational biologist's perspective ** Why systems biology? computational systems biology vs. molecular systems biology * Day 2 ** Assays, tools and techniques in PPIs: pros and cons ** Types of interactions and networks ** Data validation and integration ** Capabilities of networks * Day 3 ** A look at PPI data ** Future challenges ** Introduction to Cytoscape: brief exercise ** Networkology: data representations * Day 4 ** Exercises with iHOP, String, Bind, PreBind ** Exercises with Genecards, MINT, HPRD * Day 5 ** Summary and Q&A ==Course schedule== This course has been offered once.
